What Is Ethereum?

Technically, Ethereum is not a cryptocurrency. The word Ethereum refers to the platform. The actual term for crypto payment on the web is Ether. However, the word Ethereum is often chosen as the name for the cryptocurrency on the internet. Ethereum, like other cryptocurrencies, works with blockchain technology. There are currently about 103 million ethers in circulation. Especially in the eGambling scene, the Ether payment method is very popular and also very commonly used.

Who Is Behind Ethereum?

The founder of Ethereum is Vitalik Dimitriyevich Buterin – a colourful figure in the crypto scene. He was born on January 31, 1994, in Kolomna, Russia, and began looking deeply into blockchain technology at the age of 17. Buterin doesn’t draw much attention to himself, quite the opposite. He forgoes an official management designation and prefers to focus on the further development of online money in his foundation. The Ethereum Foundation was founded in 2014 in the Swiss crypto-valley in Zug. The first Ether was released on June 30, 2015.
